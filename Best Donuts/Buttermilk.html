Buttermilk Bar

This may seem like a dark horse to make this list, let alone make the podium, especially since there are pockets of this country that don’t even offer them. But a buttermilk bar is the augmented version of an old fashioned. It’s ameliorated both by the use of buttermilk in the cake batter in lieu of regular milk (there’s a reason pancakes are made with buttermilk!) and by its brick shape instead of a craggy ring. When your doughnut is this good, you want more of it. Pro tip: Nuke it for 20 seconds and serve with coffee or hot cocoa. Second pro tip: If the shop offers buttermilk bars in blueberry or banana, get both. Final pro tip: Do not fall for maple glazed buttermilk bars, as the maple sweetness oddly doesn’t gibe well with the overall sweetness of the cake.
